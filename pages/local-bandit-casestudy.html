Local Bandit

Find your new favorite band. Search local concerts, and create a Spotify playlist of all the performing artists top tracks all in the same place. With Local Bandit you'll never miss great bands playing near you again.

Challenge

Our team was given a challenge to create a useful web application, in two weeks. The web app had to have: third party authentication, database, stellar front end, and it actually had to address a market need.

Approach

Our team (comprised of me and Nikki) set out to do a project that met a market need. We are both avid music fans, and concert goers. In the midst of sharing our personal favorites, our conversation inevitably turned to the “ones that got away”. The concerts that we would have attended had we heard of a given band sooner. It seemed crazy that there was no one place to look up concerts happening near by.

After a flurry of Googling, we had effectively found a niche. Apps like BandsInTown would show you concerts coming up based on people you followed, and offered a list of everyone coming to town. A user could manually tap every one of them into Spotify or Youtube to see what they were all about. That was our opportunity space. There was no single app to search upcoming music, create a playlist of possibilities, and save the upcoming events that seemed interesting.

Solution

Our solution, at the end of the two week time box, allowed users to authenticate with their Spotify account, set a start and end date, select a city and state within the United states, browse events, add a potential new favorite artists top ten tracks to a combined Spotify playlist, and save events.

The Challenges

Making sure that we identified the best flow with the restrictions of the Spotify login was quite a challenge. To retrieve any playlist information of the Spotify API, the user had to be logged into their Spotify account. Ideally the user would have been able to perform a search, and only login when they wanted to add songs to their playlist. We troubleshooted this by making the Spotify login the primary method, that way the majority of our users would already be logged in prior to adding tracks to their playlist.

We chose to learn typescript on this project as well, this provided quite the little challenge as well. Setting up all the needed files for typescript required creating a bunch of interfaces. For a short project, a lot of our time went into setting Typescript up. In the long term it would pay off, due to our short timeline however, we didn’t get many of the benefits of having Typescript catch errors.

Next steps

Adding the ability to delete saved events was the only thing we were unable to get to. Its a must have ability since we allow users to save events.
