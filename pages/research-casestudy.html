Research Process

Research is integrated into every step of the design process. These are the steps taken for each project both personal and professional.

Why? Informed creativity.

By leveraging both quantitative and qualitative data in addition to customer feedback we can make faster decisions and better inform our designs.

The Process

On project kick off we follow the following framework:

  1. Discovery: customer calls, forums, create hypothesis
  2. Concept exploration: curated questions, test hypothesis, wireframes
  3. Design: design mocks based on feedback
  4. Usability & testing: calls with mocks/coded prototypes

Each phase in the process helps to clarify the problem, and serves to structure the design process. Whenever there’s lack of internal direction, or debate, we turn to our users. Each conversation adds layers of additional understanding and definition around what the finished project will be. This way we can make sure the user needs are met, and that the final designs address the root problem.

The Result

The result of applying this research framework ends in a design process that is neither linear or circular in nature. Instead it’s a structured and layered approach aimed at identifying the root of user problems and responding with innovative and pointed solutions.
