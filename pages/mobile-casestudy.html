Adobe Analytics mobile app

The Analytics Mobile application allows executives and business users to monitor the most important numbers for their companies. Anytime, anywhere.

Problem

The web application instance of Adobe Analytics is used day to day by analysts. Analysts are able to create data visualizations and tables within Analytics to better understand customer journeys and business performance. These insights are then distributed to other teams and stakeholders. There was no way for non-analysts to easily access the numbers they needed.

Approach

The approach for this mobile app project followed the outline below. The entire cross functional team would be present for each of the steps.

  1. Internal brief/project kick off
  2. Competitive analysis
  3. User interviews
  4. Low fidelity mocks
  5. Internal review
  6. Concept validation with users
  7. High fidelity mocks
  8. Coded prototype
  9. Useability testing
  10. Iteration & refinement

Design leads user interviews, creates low/high fidelity mocks, and conducts useability testing. Engineering creates a coded prototype with the help of design, in addition to spearheading the refinement efforts at the end. Product Management makes sure that the vision fits in with the company's overarching goals. A high level overview of the research process can be found here.

Solution

For the consumers: A mobile application instance of Adobe Analytics for the sole purpose of checking and tracking key metrics.

For the producers(Analysts):A new project type that gives Analysts complete creative control over the projects for the mobile app users.

The Process

Project kickoff:
This project was started with the knowledge that Adobe Analytics was a hard tool for new users to learn. The goal of the product team was simple. Set the data free. Allowing new and novice users to access and leverage the data within Adobe Analytics.

Competitive analysis:
Most of Analytics' direct competitors had a way to share numbers and visualizations in a more consumable format (like a dashboard or a mobile app). Other than a PDF export, and the ability to share full projects, there was no in between. As the design lead I conducted a competitive analysis to get an understanding of what features and offerings were working, and which were not.

View competitive analysis

User interviews:
A competitive analysis provides good context, but does not provide enough information to start designing off of. By leveraging the Adobe forums, public industry slack channels (non-adobe), and the Analytics tool we were able to set up about 10 user to interviews.

The initial user research focused on defining the pain points and needs of the consumer of the insights found within Analytics (rather than the producer of the reports).

These calls were carefully scripted (by design) to ensure that there were no leading questions. The questions would start off relatively broad, and gradually hone in on the potential solution areas.
