Project Sharing

Allows users to share projects with multiple people or groups and assign different permission levels.

Problem

Analytics has a high barrier to entry. It’s an extremely flexible tool, understanding the capabilities in combination with the business context takes time and practice. Many users are interested in the numbers, and uninterested in learning a new tool. So even though they have usernames, they rarely login themselves, and instead opt to ask one of their analysts.

Approach

Through user research and design iteration, determine what information needs to be shared when and to whom. And what format the information should be shared in to maximize how consumable it is.

Solution

Different permission levels will allow the more competent users of Analytics to democratized data. Those who are well versed enough to collaborate and add to a project can be added as a collaborator. Collaborators have all the same capabilities as the project owner, other than being able to delete the project. On the other end of the spectrum, a Viewer has a completely locked down and stripped back version of the UI. They can not add or manipulate the project in any way. This view is ideal for users who want to track the same numbers on a regular cadence.

The Process

Once we had a good idea of what the solution could be. The team made a prototype of the solution to test in person with some of our customers. We wanted to make sure that the abilities of each role matched the users expectations and needs. During the testing a lot of time was spent reviewing the Viewer project, as this was a net new UI.

The Result

Users are now able to give specific access to projects. More people have access to the data they need, and the admin doesn’t have the headache of wondering who wrote over their project.
