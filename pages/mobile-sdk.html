Mobile SDK debugger

A single tool to help the implementation and upkeep of mobile Analytics tagging. The mobile SDK debugger allows developers to test Analytics tags to ensure data is flowing into Analytics correctly.

Problem

Teams implementing, and maintaining Adobe Analytics tracking were having to do all kinds of hacky things to understand how their implementation was actually working. Especially when implementing mobile SDK’s. The screenshot below shows a set up that one of our customers sent during an initial problem validation call. They were having to use multiple programs on multiple laptops to make sure that everything was working correctly, and even then there was quite a bit of guesswork involved.

Solution

A specialized developer tool that allows users to test mobile (and all) SDKs without needing to use a proxy like Charles. Giving users the ability to see the details of Analytics hits within a matter of minutes. Eliminating the need for multiple laptops and multiple programs. Streamlining the user flow drastically, in addition to giving the users access to information (hit level metadata) that they would have had to piece together themselves.

Approach

The approach for this project followed the outline below. The entire cross functional team would be present for each of the steps.

  1. Internal brief/project kick off
  2. User interviews
  3. Research synthesis
  4. Design sprint
  5. High fidelity mocks
  6. Coded prototype
  7. Concept validation with users
  8. Useability testing
  9. Iteration & refinement

Design leads user interviews, creates low/high fidelity mocks, and conducts useability testing. Engineering creates a coded prototype with the help of design, in addition to spearheading the refinement efforts at the end. Product Management makes sure that the vision fits in with the company's overarching goals. A high level overview of the research process can be found here.

The Process

Problem validation:

As a product team we had a general idea of which problems could be addressed with this project. To ensure we were on the right track, and to better define the opportunity space we conducted a series of four initial discovery calls. The script below was written with our research team, to make sure we would get the most unbiased and candid feedback possible.

View script

Research synthesis:

Because the initial problem validation calls were so carefully scripted, the users answers are comparable to one another. Below are visuals showing the actions, pain points, and flows of each of the four companies.

The following potential opportunity spaces were used to kick off our design sprint:

  1. Equal parts art and science - We need to account for troubleshooting and discovery workflows (circular and linear)
  2. Expectations vs reality - The teams always know to some extent how things should be. How can we leverage their knowledge instead of their ability to manually sort through code?
  3. Multi-team communication - The amount of time lost trying to communicate and fix issues across teams (with screenshots and emails) is astronomical. Could we free up this time for them somehow?
  4. Mobile SDK transparency - Everyone had MANY things to say about troubleshooting the mobile SDK. Bottom line is they need to be able to see what it's doing somehow.

Design sprint:

Once engineering was able to put together a proof of concept for giving “friendly names” to the hits from Adobe Analytics we held a design sprint. We had a solid idea of which capabilities would alleviate the most user pain, thanks to the research.

We spent the next couple days honing in on what features and functionality were “must haves”, “should haves” and “nice to haves”.

We established the ideal user flow, and wireframed the general experience from end to end.

With the amount of conversation, it was easy to take the wireframes and transition them into high fidelity mocks that could validated with customers.

The Result

Thanks to the jumpstart from the user research at the beginning, the proof of concept, and the design sprint. We were able to release a full featured beta in the span of a couple months. Beta users are currently leveraging the tool daily, and giving us feedback we can use to continue development past the minimum viable product stage.
