The Labs Environment

The new Labs environment allows for gathering customer feedback on a larger scale. On a much wider range of potential solutions.

Problem

Customer feedback guides and shapes the final output during every step of the research process (discovery, concept validation, design, usability and testing). However, one product team is unable to interview all, or even the majority of the customer base.

Solution

A dedicated place within Adobe Analytics where users can test out in progress, designs, user interface improvements, and algorithms. A true win-win set up. Users get to see what the future could look like, and weigh in on what they really need. And the product teams get a more complete understanding of what the user base needs and wants.

Approach

As a team we defined the best people to talk to for our initial discovery calls. Before we jumped into design or development, we wanted to clearly understand and define the user need. We contacted customers who had been particularly vocal on the Adobe Analytics user forums about what they wanted to see in the product. We also used Analytics on Analytics to find out who our early adopters were - the people who used new features relatively close to when they were released.

After the initial discovery calls were conducted, we would regroup and synthesize our findings.

The Process

After conducting a number of user interviews, and synthesizing the results as a team three main “areas of intervention” surfaced. Summarized they amount to the below:

  1. Feedback needs to be easy to give (no forced free response)
  2. The capabilities of each prototype must be explained upfront
  3. There must be some way to filter/search prototypes

In a little more detail, number one was - contrary to what we thought going in - customers were more than happy to offer feedback at the end of testing each prototype as long as giving the feedback was easy. The second topic brought up by the customers was that of education. They wanted an easy way to jump into a prototype, and understand the context and capabilities of the proposed feature. The third was the landing experience. Not all of the prototypes would be relevant for everyone. Given that users would be engaging with this Labs environment in their free time, in addition to their responsibilities, they needed a way to quickly understand what was relevant to them.

The Result

Labs released at the beginning of summer 2019, since then we have gotten an amazing amount of feedback on our initial three prototypes. Currently the feature is only available to those with Administrator permissions, it will gradually be rolled out to the other permission groups.
